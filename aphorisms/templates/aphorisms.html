Aphorisms for the Neurodivergent

These are sayings I say to myself
in order to calm and center my consciousness
especially during episodes of withdrawl or anxiety.

 

Stop

Sit quietly

Ask for openness

Your job is to feel

Learn from breathing

Letting out is letting go, of thinking

All things pass even to die

 

Time slows

To the space of a breath

 

This Is AWARENESS

-

 

the best I can do

is to take care of myself

at my worst

 

if all I can do

is to sit

lost in confusion

then so be it

 

This Is ACCEPTANCE

-

 

Then, Arrive at an ACTION

perform healing tasks

however small

 

experience

pleasure, relaxation, comfort

 

fullfill a longing

 

prioritize by doing

and watching

what you do

-

 

if there is pain or fear

"Bring It On"

"I love pain"

"Pain sets me free"

 

Hone your awareness of wrongful mechanisms.

Accept the wrongness.

Feel the pain.

That is the way to compassion.

When it hurts to decide between the virulent possibilities--do nothing.

It's OK to feel bad.

The highest good is taking care of me.

Prioritize by doing.

Follow your nose around.

Slow down.

I'm in no hurry.

I have no future projection.

Take time to heal.

Honor suffering; it is not forever.

Look under anxiety; there is pain there.

Don't decide the little choices

let them happen

don't control

let the decisions come to you

Anxiety doesn't serve.

You would do the same anyway without it

so give way and feel under.

Turn off the clocks.

My higher power is the endless now.

It's OK to feel weak

or wrong

or small

Hold yourself in sympathy

ask for help

don't try to fix or hide

letting go leads

to well-being in the moment

I can do no wrong.

I'm not doing anything wrong.

Everything (I could do)

is already correct

Every moment is exactly perfect

including those when I feel only

only fear, shame, hyper-vigilance

use that knowledge to feel under the fear

and let go

still perfect

regardless of result

I cannot force myself to exist

in the endless now

... however ...

it is easy

Take breath

Exhale

let go all time

Ask for receptivity

"Openness Gentleness Kindness"

Shame is the language of the oppressor. Resist!

Even if—especially if—it's in your own head.

Trust in the What

and the Why will answer itself.

STOP forcing yourself to do what you should.

DO only that which you want to

that which gives you pleasure.

Notes

Awareness, Acceptance, and Action are known as the "Three A's" in Twelve Step programs.

"Bring It On," "I love pain," and "Pain sets me free come from The Tools by Phil Stutz and Barry Michels.

Openness Gentleness Kindness come from Pema Chodron, The Wisdom of No Escape.
